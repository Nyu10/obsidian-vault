Devote your time to seeing Christ

- **The Shift:** Devoting your time no longer means "attending church events." It means maintaining a **rhythym of prayer and reflection** when you're working 60+ hours a week.
    
- **The "Magi" Application:** Just as the Magi traveled through a "secular" desert to find Christ, you are "pursuing" Him through a secular career. If you can keep your integrity and your "inner life" intact while scaling a company, that is a high level of pursuit.


- **The Shift:** The sermon argues that the Magi gave Gold, Frankincense, and Myrrh because those were their most valuable assets.
    
- **The Application:** Devoting your treasures doesn't just mean "tithing" your salary. It means using your **influence, your network, and your skills** to help others. It means not let your "equity" become your identity. If the startup succeeds, you treat the wealth as a tool for service rather than a scorecard for your worth.


This is the hardest part. The "posture" of a startup founder or early employee is often one of **self-reliance, ego, and "hustle."**

- **The Shift:** The sermon says the Magi "fell down and worshiped." This is an act of **submission**.
    
- **The Application:** You can be a high-performer in SF while maintaining a "submissive posture" toward Christ. This means:
    
    - Refusing to lie to investors or cut ethical corners.
        
    - Treating your coworkers with a dignity that isn't tied to their productivity.
        
    - Recognizing that your success (or failure) doesn't change your standing with God.
