1. **Does this action violate the principle of loving my neighbor?** If my actions deprive someone else of their livelihood (streaming) or endanger someone else's safety (jaywalking), it impacts the community.
    
2. **Does this action reflect integrity?** Integrity is often defined as doing the right thing even when no one is watching and even when the action seems trivial.

- **Struggling with sin:** Where a person recognizes an action is wrong, feels conviction about it, and attempts to align their life differently.
- **Rationalizing sin:** Where a person minimizes or ignores their behavior, convincing themselves it "doesn't matter" to God just to avoid the discomfort of changing.


- **Library Systems (Digital & Physical):** You mentioned living in Ann Arbor—the **Ann Arbor District Library (AADL)** and the **University of Michigan Library** system are world-class. Many libraries offer access to **Libby** or **Hoopla**, which provide thousands of free e-books and audiobooks legally. They often have partnerships with major publishers, so you are essentially "borrowing" them, and the author still gets compensated through the library's licensing agreements.
    
- **Open Access/Public Domain:** For non-fiction and classic literature, sites like **Project Gutenberg** or **Standard Ebooks** offer massive amounts of content that is legally free because the copyright has expired or the author released it for free.

**The Slippery Slope:** If you decide that you are allowed to cheat because you were "wronged" elsewhere, it’s a very small jump to justifying other things. It’s the same logic that leads people to think, "My boss underpays me, so I’ll just take some office supplies," or "The government is corrupt, so I don't need to report this income."
