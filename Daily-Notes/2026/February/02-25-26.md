**_makrothumia_** ($μακροθυμία$). 
- **Makros:** Means "long" or "large."
- **Thumos:** Means "temper," "passion," or "heat."

long tempered 

**Exodus 34:6:** When God describes Himself to Moses, He says He is "The Lord, the Lord God, merciful and gracious, **longsuffering** ($arek$ $appayim$ in Hebrew), and abounding in goodness and truth."

1. **Hupomone (Endurance/Patience):** Usually refers to staying strong under **circumstances** (like a heavy load or a long wait).
2. **Makrothumia (Longsuffering):** Usually refers to staying strong in the face of **suffering or people.**
    
**The Deep Insight:** You cannot have "longsuffering" unless you are, well, **suffering.** If life were easy and you were always "happy," this fruit of the Spirit would never grow. It is a "stress-tested" fruit.

- **The Spirit vs. The Body:** If someone dies of a heart attack, we don't say they lacked "Joy." If someone's brain—which is a physical organ—eventually gives out under the weight of 20 years of depression, it is a **physical collapse of a broken body in a broken world.**
- **The Comfort:** The "Fruit of the Spirit" (Longsuffering) is what kept that person going for those 20 years. The fact that they lasted 20 years in a world they didn't want to be in is actually a testament to a **massive amount of spiritual strength**, not a lack of it.
