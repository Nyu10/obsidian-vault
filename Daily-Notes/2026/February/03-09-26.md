Zechariah is deeply rooted in a very specific, "boring" historical moment: the return from the Babylonian exile in **520 BC**.

- **Chapters 1–8:** Encouraging the people to finish the Temple.
    
- **Chapters 9–14:** Wild, futuristic prophecies about global war, a shepherd being struck, and the Mount of Olives splitting in half.


For 70 years, the Jews were stuck in **Babylon**. But then, the **Persian Empire** (led by Cyrus the Great) conquered Babylon.
- **The Policy Change:** Unlike the Babylonians who wanted everyone to assimilate, the Persians had a "Return to Home" policy. They let the Jews go back to Jerusalem to rebuild.
- **The Catch:** The Jews weren't "free"; they were a tiny, poor province within the massive Persian machine. They were constantly worried that if they stepped out of line, the Persian King would crush them.
- **The Theology:** Zechariah has to remind the people that even though Persia _looks_ like the boss, God is the "Lord of Hosts" (the Commander of the Heavenly Armies) who actually runs the show.
