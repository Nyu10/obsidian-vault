Keller would likely point you straight to **Mark 7:11-13**. In that passage, Jesus rebukes religious people who took money meant for their aging parents and dedicated it to the Temple (calling it "Corban," or a gift to God).

- **The Irony:** Your mom did exactly what the Pharisees did—she prioritized a "religious" project (the prayer room) over her actual biblical duty (saving so she wouldn't be a burden).
    
- **Keller’s Take:** He would say that "spiritualizing" a renovation while neglecting financial stability is not true piety; it’s a form of **idolatry**. God does not want a prayer room funded by the future bankruptcy of a family.
    
- **The Application:** Keller would likely tell you that while you have a duty to ensure she doesn't starve or go homeless, you are _not_ obligated to maintain the lifestyle she chose through incompetence.
