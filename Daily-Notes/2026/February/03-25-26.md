**Chapters 1–8** were written by the "historical" Zechariah around **520–518 BCE**. However, **Chapters 9–14** (often called _Second Zechariah_ or _Deutero-Zechariah_)


Chapter 9:13 explicitly mentions "your sons, O Greece." In 520 BCE, Greece wasn't a world power yet. This suggests this part was written closer to the 330s BCE, around the time of **Alexander the Great**.

- **Stylistic Shift:** It switches from prose visions to intense Hebrew poetry.
    
- **Authorship:** Many scholars believe these chapters were written by an anonymous author (or authors) who were "disciples" of Zechariah’s school, updating his message for a new generation facing Greek oppression rather than Persian reconstruction.
